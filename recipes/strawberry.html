Simple Strawberry Topping

So easy, even you could do it!

This is a dead-simple strawberry topping recipe absolutely anyone could whip up to impress anyone. Taken from Natasha's Kitchen.

This recipe goes great on cheesecake, pancakes, cake, or anything else you might put syrup or fruit sauce on. It's simple and a little tart, and compliments very rich desserts quite well.

Required ingredients:

  • 1 lb. Strawberries, rinsed, hulled, and thickly sliced
  • ⅓ cup granulated white sugar
  • 1 Tbsp lemon juice (½ fresh lemon squeezed)

Directions:

  1. Wash, hull, and slice strawberries

    Make sure they're nice and clean, with all of the leaves, and most of the top removed. You want the strawberries to look like little hollow cups.

    If you're feeling a little lazy, you can get away with just slicing the whole top, just enough to get rid of all of the stem.

    You want to slice them around ¼-⅓ inch thick, or around there. Still fairly thick, you should be looking to get maybe 3-6 slices per strawberry, depending on the size.

  2. In a medium saucepan: combine sliced strawberries, lemon juice, and sugar, bring to a boil, stirring occasionally

    Don't worry about mashing the slices, they'll release plenty of juice on their own with heat and time. Just move them around so the ones against the bottom don't burn.

  3. Reduce heat and simmer 20-23 minutes or until sauce is thickened, stirring occasionally

    Sauce should be a little thinner than the viscosity of maple syrup. If it still acts like water if you give the pan a little swirl, give it another 2-3 minutes simmering at a time until it's done.

  4. Remove from heat and allow to cool to room temperature

    The sauce will get thicker as it cools, especially if you refrigerate it before serving.

Serving

Goes great with this cheesecake recipe, cake, or a tablespoon! No matter how much you make, you'll wish you made more!
