Chef John's Baked Potato Puffs

Pommes Dauphine without the deep fryer

A riff on the classic deep-fried French recipe of Pommes Dauphine, these potato puffs are baked in the oven, making them accesible for anyone to make! Somewhere between baked potato and pastry, they have a wonderful texture and a terrific flavor! This recipe was taken from Chef John's Baked Potato Puffs

Required ingredients:

  • 4 small Yukon gold potatoes, cleaned and quartered
  • ½ cup water
  • ¼ cup butter
  • kosher salt to taste
  • 60 grams flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper
  • 1 pinch freshly grated nutmeg

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 450 degrees F, butter 24 mini muffin cups

  2. Place quartered potatoes in a large lot, add enough salted water to cover them

    You're safe to add a liberal shake of salt to the water, around 2-3 tablespoons. Use the cheap salt, most of it's going out with the water

  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for about 15 minutes

    You'll know the potatoes are done if a knife can go in cleanly and easily. You don't want them totally crumbly.

  4. Drain and mash the potatoes, set aside

    You don't want the potatoes absolutely piping hot for the next step, so if you can, try to use a fairly big mixing bowl, and spread the potatoes out as best you can inside it.

  5. Combine water and butter in a saucepan over medium heat and season with salt. Heat until butter melts and starts to bubble. Stir in flour until mixture pulls away from the pan and comes together into a dough.

    Try to spread the flour as evenly as possible over the water to minimize clumping

  6. Transfer pastry dough to a bowl. Spread in the bowl and let cool, about 5 minutes. Mix in eggs using a spatula until dough is very soft and sticky.

    You only want the dough warm, otherwise it will cook the egg yolks before they can properly incorporate, ruining the texture.

  7. Add 2 cups of the mashed potatoes and mix until well combined. Add a pinch of salt, cayenne pepper, and nutmeg. Give it one last mix.

    You don't want to massively overmix it, but the danger isn't nearly as severe as it might be for a cheesecake or souffle.

  8. Scoop potato dough into the greased muffin cups; bake for about 20 minutes, or until golden brown and puffed.

    Let them rest for 1 minute before removing to a wire rack. Let them cool 5 minutes more from there. Trust me on that one.

Serving

This dish goes well anywhere, but truly shines beside a nicely seared steak. Use it any time you would serve another hot potato dish, or simply paired with a savory dipping sauce as an appetizer or hors d'oeuvres.
